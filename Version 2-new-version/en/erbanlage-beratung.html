suddenly responsibility becomes

An inheritance changes many things at once, financially and emotionally. What sounded like returns and growth yesterday is now a question of security, value preservation and good decisions. It is precisely in this phase that uncertainty often arises instead of clarity.

1

The special challenge after an inheritance

An inheritance changes your financial situation suddenly, often in a phase in which you are still building up your wealth.

Typical starting points:

  • Your previous strategy was return-oriented and prone to fluctuations
  • Suddenly, value retention becomes just as important as growth
  • The sum seems large, the responsibility even greater
  • Fear of making wrong decisions leads to stagnation

We know this situation and create clarity with a well-founded, long-term sustainable strategy.

2

Our approach: Planning creates security

Before we invest, we create an overview and build a plan from it. Together we analyze:

  • your overall situation (assets, income, obligations)
  • Liquidity requirements, time horizons and reserve logic
  • Risk tolerance and security requirements
  • Goals, values ​​and family wishes
  • tax framework conditions (within the legal framework)

Only when these points are clear will the basis for a reliable genetic strategy be created, without time pressure and without product specifications.

3

Strategies for wealth that should last

Our genetic advice pursues two goals at the same time:

  • long-term asset growth
  • Limit downside risks, especially in times of crisis

That means: not maximum returns at any price, but a structured concept with clear rules that reduces unnecessary risks and develops assets in a stable manner.

4

Our multi-asset approach

A single asset class rarely delivers stability and growth at the same time. That's why we diversify consciously, independently and comprehensibly:

  • Broadly diversified ETFs and, if necessary, selected funds across several asset classes
  • additional building blocks (e.g. real estate) if they make strategic sense
  • clear weighting and a portfolio that works together as a system

The result: a balanced multi-asset strategy that enables growth without “all-or-nothing” risk.
